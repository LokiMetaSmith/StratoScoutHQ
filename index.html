The Problem

Emergency managers lack geospatial intelligence that is wide enough, detailed enough, and fast enough to act on.

Emergency teams need current, detailed visibility across large areas before conditions turn critical. But existing data is often too limited or too delayed to show where risk is developing, leaving incident commanders without a clear understanding of what is happening on the ground or where to act first.

"Data from a high-altitude UAS could be useful for incident commanders potential to evaluate spread possibilities." Meteorologist, NWS Tulsa (2026)

The Solution

High-altitude drones that turn high-risk conditions into usable intelligence within hours.

StratoScout provides autonomous, high-altitude UAS as a service for emergency managers who need wide-area visibility before and during high-risk conditions. The system gathers current vegetation, heat, terrain, and location data, then turns it into maps and insights emergency teams can act on.

📡

Wide-Area Coverage at Speed

A single flight covers nearly 100 miles in length and up to 3 miles wide, giving emergency managers a comprehensive picture of large areas in hours rather than days.

💰

Low-Cost, Retrievable Drones

Autonomous and fully retrievable, StratoScout drones are built to keep operational costs low, making wide-area aerial intelligence practical and repeatable.

📍

Actionable Geospatial Data

Raw sensor data is processed into clear maps, risk zones, and location-specific insights that emergency teams can use directly, without needing to interpret complex datasets.

🤖

AI-Assisted Intelligence

AI helps distill aerial and weather data into prioritized alerts and spread indicators, so commanders spend less time sorting through information and more time acting on it.
